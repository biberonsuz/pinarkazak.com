PINAR KAZAK WORKS ABOUT

Stuck Figures are a style of drawing inspired from the feeling of feeling stuck. First developed as drawings of stick figures stuck between text, they later developed into RISO prints, ink stamps, and later an installation. The style of drawing was developed into different forms, exploring materiality, colour, space and the expression of the space. These forms include; Painting, coasters, plant pots, tote bags, shoulder bags, stamps, prints and an installation.

Specifically the installation plays with the idea of color and opacity, and speaks of the uneasy feeling of being stuck in an abundance of space. The installation consisted of three transparent sheets of 1 x 2 meters. By having the transparent canvases approximately human sized, it reflected the idea of the expression of feeling stuck with the human body, by shrinking in pose and in size.

The same idea was later explored again using the painting medium, this time filling up the canvas, playing more with the idea of space in terms of the space outside the canvas, as opposed to the opacity of the canvas. It was also later developed into a tote bag, going back to the idea of transparency, and later developed into transparent and colourful coasters.
